Waffle Sandwitch

10 minutes Serves 2 200 cal
#Egg #Bacon #Cheese #Sandwitch

Transform your breakfast routine with these delicious waffle sandwiches! Perfect for a quick morning meal or weekend brunch, these sandwiches combine crispy waffles with eggs, bacon, and cheese for a satisfying start to your day. Add nuts and fresh fruit for extra crunch and sweetness!

Ingredients

For the Waffles:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Filling:

  • 8 slices bacon
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Optional Toppings:

  • Maple syrup
  • Sliced almonds or pistachios
  • Fresh apple slices
  • Raisins
  • Fresh berries

Instructions

Step 1: Make Waffle Batter

In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. In another bowl, beat eggs, milk, oil, and vanilla. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed (some small lumps are okay).

Step 2: Cook Bacon

Cook bacon in a large skillet until crispy, about 8-10 minutes. Remove to paper towels to drain. Break each slice in half.

Step 3: Cook Eggs

In the same skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Crack eggs and cook to desired doneness (over-medium recommended for sandwiches). Season with salt and pepper.

Step 4: Make Waffles

Preheat waffle iron. Pour about 3/4 cup batter for each waffle. Cook until golden brown and crispy.

Step 5: Assemble

Place cheese on hot waffle, top with egg, bacon, and optional toppings. Cover with second waffle. Serve immediately.

Nutritional Information

Per Sandwich:

  • Calories: 200
  • Protein: 14g
  • Carbohydrates: 28g
  • Fat: 16g
  • Fiber: 2g
  • Sodium: 540mg

Tips

  • Don't overmix the waffle batter - small lumps are good!
  • Make waffles extra crispy for better sandwich structure
  • Cook eggs over-medium for perfect yolk consistency
  • Warm maple syrup before serving
  • Try different cheese varieties like Swiss or Gouda

Storage

Extra waffles can be frozen for up to 3 months. Reheat in a toaster for best results. Assembled sandwiches are best eaten immediately but can be wrapped and refrigerated for up to 2 hours. Reheat in oven at 350Â°F for 5-7 minutes to maintain crispiness.
